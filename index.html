Literary Studies at KAIST

Literature is a timeless window into the human experience.

Literary Studies at KAIST is the research lab dedicated to exploring the intersection of literature and cutting-edge methodologies, including computational analysis and digital humanities. This innovative lab aims to bridge the rich traditions of literary inquiry with the advancements in data-driven research, fostering a multidisciplinary approach to understanding texts. Researchers at the lab analyze classic and contemporary works using tools such as natural language processing, machine learning, and Bayesian modeling, uncovering patterns and insights that traditional methods might overlook.

The lab also emphasizes the study of translation, focusing on how cultural, historical, and linguistic factors shape the interpretation of literary works. By examining diverse translations of classics like Jane Eyre or Kafka's writings, the team investigates shifts in meaning, style, and reader perception over time.

With its forward-looking approach, Literary Studies at KAIST is redefining how we engage with literature, creating a vibrant space for innovation and scholarly collaboration.
